## A taste: three selection methods

One of the things the guide unpacks more clearly than the app itself is the difference between the three wheel selection methods. Here's the short version:

**Full Wheel** generates every possible combination of your chosen numbers, available on all plans. Play all of them for complete coverage, or reduce the ticket count to take a random subset of the full wheel. A "repick" button on the Tickets screen pulls a fresh random subset whenever you want one — same numbers, different sample of combinations.

**Broad Coverage** (Pro) uses a stride algorithm to select a smaller set of tickets that spreads your numbers evenly across the full combination space. Fewer tickets, even coverage. The repick button works here too, for a different evenly-spaced set without changing your underlying picks.

**Abbreviated Wheel** (Elite) uses pre-computed mathematical wheel designs that guarantee a minimum match level when enough of your numbers are drawn. Fewer tickets than a full wheel, with provable coverage guarantees.

Three methods, three approaches to the same goal — better coverage of the numbers you pick. The guide explains the strengths of each so you can match the method to how you like to play.

## A taste: actually checking your wins

Checking tickets by eye is fine for one or two, but it gets old fast when you've played a full wheel. The guide walks through how the app's Win Checker handles it for you: enter the drawing's winning numbers, hit a button, and every ticket gets compared automatically with matches highlighted on each one and a result summary at the top.

For Powerball and Mega Millions, prize tiers apply automatically. For custom games, you fill in the prize amounts per match level and the app does the totaling. Results save back to your history, so you can track winnings over time.
